I'll list the flaws in order of most fundamental to least.

### 1. Teachers

All other issues listed here stem from this. We currently use a teacher to convey information to students live, this causes teachers to keep repeating the same information over and over again, which makes them make mistakes, and wastes their time and energy. We have invented a tool that allows for transfer of information without a person talking, its called writing.

It also makes schools expensive, and limits the school's curriculum to the available teachers. Imagine if we didn't need 30 teachers per school, instead just having written content distributed to students, with that content not being locked in a classroom, but publically available and thus easy to correct, update.

### 2. De-individualised learning.

We learn by creating connections between concepts. Everyone has different knowledge, it is a product of an individual's life expierience. Thus people will learn ideas at different rates.

School fails to account for this, giving everyone the same material, at the same rate. Actually efficient learning methods are tailored to the indiviual, such as spaced repetition software that specifically accounts for how easily one can anwser a card, adjusting the knowledge given based on an algorithm.

Our current model completely fails to account for this fundament of learning.
